Why your data center matters

For many businesses, the data center is still the heart of operations: file servers, line-of-business applications, databases, backups and security controls all live there. Poor design or aging hardware quickly becomes visible as slowness, outages and security incidents.

RomanoTI Solutions designs and maintains data center environments with a clear goal: availability first, security by design, and predictable costs.

What we help you improve

Availability

Always-on infrastructure

Redundant hosts, storage and network paths to keep your core services running even during failures.

Performance

Faster apps & databases

Right-sizing CPU, RAM and storage tiers so users feel systems are responsive, not “laggy”.

Security

Segmented & monitored

Network segmentation, hardened hypervisors and integration with SOC / logging platforms.

Data Center & Virtualization services

Architecture & Design

We review your current environment and design a modern architecture based on your size and budget.

  • Rack, power and cooling planning
  • Server, storage and network topology
  • Segmentation between users, servers and management
  • High-availability design (clusters, failover, load balancing)

Virtualization Platforms

Consolidate workloads on secure, well-configured virtualization platforms.

  • Hyper-V, VMware or KVM environments
  • Template and image management
  • Resource pools and capacity planning
  • Secure remote access for administrators

Backups & Disaster Recovery

We design backup and recovery strategies you can actually test and rely on.

  • Image-level and file-level backups
  • Off-site / cloud replication
  • RPO / RTO objectives aligned with the business
  • Disaster Recovery runbooks and testing

Monitoring & Capacity

Visibility on health, performance and growth, integrated with your NOC / SOC when needed.

  • Host, VM and storage monitoring
  • Alerts integrated with ticketing or on-call rotations
  • Capacity and growth reports for management
  • Recommendations to avoid unplanned outages
